5 Signs Your Driveway Needs Repaving

Your driveway takes a beating year after year — from heavy vehicles to harsh weather. Knowing when it's time to repave can save you money and prevent bigger problems down the road. Here are five signs to watch for:

  1. Widespread cracking. A few hairline cracks are normal, but when they spread across the surface or form "alligator" patterns, the base layer may be failing.
  2. Potholes forming. Potholes aren't just an eyesore — they damage vehicles and create tripping hazards. They signal that moisture has penetrated beneath the surface.
  3. Drainage issues. If water pools on your driveway instead of running off, the grading has shifted and the surface may be sinking unevenly.
  4. Faded, gray appearance. Fresh asphalt is rich black. When it turns gray throughout, the binder has oxidized and the surface is brittle and vulnerable.
  5. Crumbling edges. Edges that break apart indicate the asphalt is past its useful life and patching won't hold.

If you notice two or more of these signs, it's time to call a professional. Contact us for a free estimate — we'll assess your driveway and recommend the most cost-effective solution.

Asphalt vs. Tar & Chip: Which Is Right for You?

Choosing a driveway surface is one of the biggest decisions homeowners face. The two most popular options in our area are hot-mix asphalt and tar & chip (also called chip seal). Here's how they compare:

Hot-Mix Asphalt

  • Smooth, uniform finish — ideal for a polished look
  • Excellent for high-traffic driveways and parking lots
  • Easy to sealcoat for long-term maintenance
  • Typically lasts 15–20 years with proper care

Tar & Chip

  • Textured, rustic appearance that blends with rural settings
  • Lower upfront cost than hot-mix asphalt
  • Good traction — less slippery in wet conditions
  • No sealcoating required, but may need a fresh chip layer over time

Which Should You Choose?

If you want a sleek, modern look and plan to maintain it with regular sealcoating, asphalt is the way to go. If you prefer a natural look, have a longer rural driveway, or want to save on upfront costs, tar & chip is an excellent choice. How to Protect Your Driveway Through Winter

West Virginia winters bring freezing temperatures, snow, and ice — all of which can damage your driveway. A little preparation in the fall goes a long way toward extending its life.

Before Winter Hits

  • Sealcoat in late summer or early fall. A fresh sealcoat creates a waterproof barrier that prevents moisture from seeping into cracks and freezing.
  • Fill cracks now. Even small cracks expand dramatically through freeze-thaw cycles. Filling them before winter prevents costly repairs in spring.
  • Clean the surface. Remove debris, leaves, and dirt. Organic material holds moisture against the asphalt and accelerates deterioration.

During Winter

  • Use a plastic-edge snow shovel. Metal blades can gouge and scratch asphalt surfaces.
  • Go easy on rock salt. While salt won't damage asphalt directly, it accelerates freeze-thaw cycles. Use calcium chloride or sand for traction instead.
  • Don't let snow pile up on edges. Heavy snow banks along driveway edges can cause crumbling as they melt and refreeze.
